  Technical expertise is more legible, rewarded, and easier to defend than
  lived, adaptive wisdom—creating incentives to stay on high ground.

Clean frameworks and technical skills are easy to credential, measure, and
sell. The messy, contextual wisdom that comes from deep engagement is harder
to package—even though it's often what's most needed for real change.

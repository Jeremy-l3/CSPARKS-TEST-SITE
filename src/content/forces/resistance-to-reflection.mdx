  People often resist reflection due to discomfort, time pressure, or lack
  of structure—even when they know it would help.

Looking honestly at what's working and what isn't can be uncomfortable.
Combined with the pressure to "do" rather than "think," reflection often
gets skipped—leading to repeated mistakes and missed opportunities.
